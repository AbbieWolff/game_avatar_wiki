Avatar Appearance

Players usually care a great deal about their avatarâ€™s appearance.

They often spend hours carefully creating avatars, selecting their gender, age, ethnicity, clothing, accessories and more stylistic considerations, to present their desired image in games.

As with real-world personal style preferences and motivations for *self-representation and impression management*, gamers have specific motivations for their avatar appearance choices.

Overarching motivations for avatar creation
Researchers found that player motivations for avatar creation could be grouped into four categories2:
  • virtual exploration, taking advantage of the digital, immersive environments to do things otherwise impossible or difficult;
  • social navigation, maneuvering the social dynamic in these complex worlds to make friends and build reputation;
  • contextual adaptation, adjusting and responding to the socio-cultural contexts;
  • and identity representation, portraying either the actual or the ideal self.
Opposite-gender avatars
Players often choose to play as opposite-gender avatars during avatar creation.

One study found that 23% of male players and 7% of female players chose opposite-gender avatars.1

Male players with female avatars used more emotional phrases and emoticons than male players with same-gender avatars. They also moved their avatars backwards and jumped more often than female players playing female avatars did.1

These behaviors show that men reinforce idealized notions of feminine appearance and communication while playing as female avatars.1

Thus, selecting avatar gender may be less a matter of identity expression, and more a strategic selection of available multi-modal codes that players take up in their navigation of this digital space.1
