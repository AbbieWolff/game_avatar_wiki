Diversity Representation

Overview
Research shows that women and minorities face significant risk of harassment and threats of violence online, especially in response to speaking out about sexism/racism, etc.:

Kamp explains, "The Internet isn't the discreet [sic] entity we'd like to think it is. It's more a way of mediating our connections to other people. So it's no surprise, really, that all of the unpleasant aspects of presenting as female have made the leap smoothly from "real life" to online.” 1

Some researchers are using virtual reality technology to help prevent sexual harassment and assault through assertiveness training.2
Avoiding Harassment & Finding Acceptance Online
Anonymity can be both a mask for harassers to wear to protect themselves from consequences of harassment or misbehavior online.

However, online anonymity can also be an opportunity for identity exploration in a safer space than the real world. Virtual worlds like Second Life can offer players a chance to explore their sexuality and gender identity in a “place” where their safety is not threatened by such exploration--unlike many actual places around the world where their real lives could be in danger for such identity exploration.
