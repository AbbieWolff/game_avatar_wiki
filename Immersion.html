Immersion

Definition
In video gaming, immersion -- sometimes known as presence -- is the subjective feeling players experience when they are engrossed in the virtual world of a video game. That is, when they feel that they are inhabiting that world.
There are several types of immersion/presence
  • Self-presence: when the player feels as though s/he is the avatar 1
  • Social presence: when a player is inhabiting the same social space as another person 2
  • Physical/spatial presence: when a player feels as though s/he is inhabiting the avatar/virtual world 2
Conditions for and increasing immersion
  • Player wants to play the game
  • Player is physically comfortable and not distracted by the real world
  • Player is experienced in gaming/not overly frustrated with interface
  • Player identifies with the avatar
  • Perceived similarity between avatar appearance and player appearance
  • Realistic behavior by Non-Player Characters (NPCs) ?
Effects of presence on real-life behavior
Researchers have demonstrated the vast possibilities of behavior modification using avatars in virtual worlds. Even avatar appearance can change both the player's behavior and the behavior of those who interact with him/her. A case study illustrates the confusion that can occur when people become immersed in a virtual world in a study conducted by the University of Barcelona.
