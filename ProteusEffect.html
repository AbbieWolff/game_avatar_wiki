The Proteus Effect

Definition
The Proteus Effect occurs when avatar appearance leads to behavior change.

Researchers believe it is due to *immersiveness* of interactive *virtual reality* (e.g. games), rather than passive representations (e.g. films or books) that causes the Proteus Effect to flourish. 2

Behavior Modification
There are numerous examples of *behavior change* motivated by *avatar appearance* in the research literature on avatars.

For instance, seeing a virtual representation of oneself exercising can motivate players to exercise themselves, in real-life. 2

Therapists have found that the Proteus Effect increases the effectiveness of *virtual reality therapy* with patients struggling with body dissatisfaction, substance abuse issues, and more.
