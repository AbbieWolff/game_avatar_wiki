The Proteus Effect

Definition
The Proteus Effect occurs when avatar appearance leads to behavior change.

Researchers believe it is due to the immersiveness of interactive virtual realities (e.g. games), rather than passive representations (e.g. films or books) that causes the Proteus Effect to flourish. 1

Behavior Modification
There are numerous examples of behavior change motivated by avatar appearance in the research literature on avatars.

For instance, seeing a virtual representation of oneself exercising can motivate players to exercise themselves, in real-life. 1

Therapists have found that the Proteus Effect increases the effectiveness of virtual reality therapy with patients struggling with body dissatisfaction, substance abuse issues, and more.

Examples
The height and attractiveness of players' avatars can influence their behavior.

Players with taller avatars behave in a more dominant manner. Conversely, players with shorter avatars behave in a more submissive manner.2

Similarly, players who perceive their avatars as less attractive than others' stand farther away from other avatars in conversation than their more attractive peers do. 2
