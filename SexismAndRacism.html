Women are treated differently in MMOs
A study on the interaction between player gender, avatar gender and social communication and behavior in Massively Multiplayer Online (MMO) video games found that1:
  • Women actually played more hours and more characters than men did.
  • Women experience sexism within the virtual world.
  • Women perceive that such games are “made” for men....
  • Men often assume all other players are also men unless proven otherwise. However, female players who do convince the male counterpart of her gender face further discrimination.
  • Male players often will downplay females’ abilities or engage in courtship.
  • Much in the same way that men gender-bend to receive assistance, women sometimes report gender-bending to avoid the simple hassles of dealing with the issue of gender altogether.


Misrepresentation influences real-world behaviors & attitudes towards women
Compared to passive representations of characters in other entertainment mediums, the immersive element of video games leads to heightened, more acute real-world effects.2:

Playing an immersive game featuring sexualized, negatively stereotyped and/or objectified female characters makes male players more likely to sexually harass women, express sexism, and accept myths about rape. Female players who encounter the same type of representations of women feel less confident and less capable of achieving their goals. Players of all genders think women are less intellectually capable after playing a game that has such an unfavorable representation of women.2

Racist representations influence real-world attitudes and behaviors
"Using avatars that conform to common racial stereotypes can intensify prejudicial attitudes."3

After playing video games in which black characters were perceived as violent, such as Saints Row 2, players are measurably more aggressive and have a higher measured level of implicit racism.3

This effect also occurs with players who encounter frequent portrayals of police brutality and of sexually objectified and stereotyped women in video games; they can become measurably more fearful of police and less respectful towards women over time.3
