Racism & Sexism

Women Are Treated Differently in MMOs
A study on the interaction between player gender, avatar gender and social communication and behavior in Massively Multiplayer Online (MMO) video games found that:1
  • Women experience sexism within the virtual world.
  • Women actually played more hours and more characters than men did.
  • Women perceive that such games are “made” for men.
  • Men often assume all other players are also men unless proven otherwise. However, female players who do convince their male counterpart of their gender face further discrimination.
  • Male players will often downplay females’ abilities or engage in courtship.
  • Much in the same way that men gender-bend to receive assistance, women sometimes report gender-bending to avoid the simple hassles of dealing with the issue of gender altogether.
Misrepresentation Influences Real-World Behaviors & Attitudes towards Women
By playing an immersive game featuring sexualized, negatively stereotyped and/or objectified female characters, male players become more likely to2
  • sexually harass women
  • express sexism
  • accept myths about rape


Female players who encounter the same type of representations of women feel less confident and less capable of achieving their goals. 2

Players of all genders think women are less intellectually capable after playing a game that has such an unfavorable representation of women.2
Racist Representations Influence Real-World Attitudes and Behaviors
A recent study found that "using avatars that conform to common racial stereotypes can intensify prejudicial attitudes."3

After playing video games in which black characters were perceived as violent, such as Saints Row 2, study participants were found to be more aggressive and to have a higher measured level of implicit racism. 3

The researcher, Jacobs, also noted that this effect can also be shown with players who encounter frequent portrayals of police brutality and of sexually objectified and stereotyped women in video games.3

Players of such games can become measurably more fearful of police and less respectful towards women over time.3
