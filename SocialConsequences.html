Social Consequences

Definition
Actions do not exist in a vacuum. There are social consequences for taking actions that defy social norms.

Both social norms and subsequent social consequences depend upon the community.

Virtual social norms are, unsurprisingly, similar to real-world social norms; after all, virtual communication is simply another way of interacting with other people.

Miscommunication is abundant online because, without body language cues, itâ€™s more difficult to know whether someone is trustworthy. Trust and reputation are very important to relationship formation in MMOGs (Massively Multiplayer Online Games).

Anonymous users may not see the social consequences of their actions or not care because they believe the consequences will not transfer to the real world or to their persistent identities. However, all social communication--even online--inherently includes the chance of consequences.
