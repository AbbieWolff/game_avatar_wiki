Social Norms

Definition
Social norms are social rules for appropriate behavior that are formed by groups, communities, and cultures of people.

Sometimes, social norms for virtual behavior can deviate from real-world social norms due to the nature of virtual environments and communities.1
Group Identification
Users who identify with their avatars and who identify with the virtual community in an online game are more likely to continue to play the game and to feel more satisfied with, efficacious in, and trusting of the virtual world and its community.
Similarity & Conformity
Group identity can be strengthened when people wear similar clothing or styles.

Researchers sought to find out if being assigned similar-looking, or the same, avatars in virtual groups made users more or less likely to identify with their groups and to conform to group social norms in virtual environments. They found that similarity in virtual appearance among group members led to2:
  • increased virtual group identity
  • players with diverse backgrounds found it easier to communicate with each other
  • players found it easier to identify with one another
  • Decreased prejudice in interactions
Virtual Gender Norms
In real life, most people are expected to identify as one gender and continue to present themselves as that gender in all facets of life. Their identity in the real world is expected to include identifying with that one, relatively static gender identity: male, female, or another variation.

Conversely, in virtual worlds, it has become a social norm for some players (of all genders) to adopt an opposite-gender or non-gendered avatar.

Such changes to avatar appearance can lead players to behave differently in virtual worlds. This is known as the Proteus Effect

Researchers studying the differences in social communication behaviors of male and female players who played opposite-gender avatars in online video games found that men reinforced idealized notions of feminine appearance and communication while playing as female game characters. 3

Gender and other forms of diversity representation have noticeable effects upon numerous facets of gamers’ behavior--even subsequent real-world behaviors and attitudes.
