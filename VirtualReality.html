Virtual & Augmented Reality

Virtual Reality
Virtual Reality (VR) ? technology blocks all visual information from the real world and replaces it with an immersive experience of a virtual world that, ideally, fills the entire visual field of view.

Most current-generation virtual reality products include a head-mounted display (HMD). This display rests on the heads of users. It blocks their view of the real world and replaces it with stereoscopic goggles/glasses. 1

Stereoscopic, or 3D, vision enables depth perception in virtual reality by providing slightly different views for each eye, similar to how the human perceptual system creates depth perception using the difference between the mismatched views of human eyes.

The goal of VR is to temporarily replace reality with virtual reality. 2
Augmented Reality
Similar to VR, many new Augmented Reality (AR) technology products include goggles or glasses. However, rather than replacing the entire field of view of the user (as in VR) with virtual reality, in AR, virtual elements are superimposed over users’ view of the real world. 3

The goal of AR is to improve upon reality, without replacing it.
How it works
Virtual head-mounted displays (HMDs) utilize current technologies 4 that make it possible to
  • faithfully recreate 2D visual representations of films and games on ubiquitous high-definition screens.
  • use low-tech, cheap glasses in “3D” movie theater showings to see a semblance of 3D effects.

These technologies also utilize multiple location, movement and eye gaze tracking sensors, like those present in most modern smartphones and tablets, to keep users’ view up-to-date with their actual location. Some also offer multi-directional sound as part of VR and AR headsets. 1 4 5

Potential uses for both virtual and augmented reality are numerous. Scientists and military personnel have been developing, testing and even using virtual and augmented reality technology for years in laboratories. 1

In the future, researchers hope to produce voice-, gaze-, or mind-controlled VR/AR; 1 6 as well as provide immersion for touch (haptics), smell, and taste in virtual reality. 1
