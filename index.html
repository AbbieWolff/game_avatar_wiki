Identity Exploration via Video Game Avatars

Many facets of the human experience occur in virtual worlds when players explore their identities through video game avatars. For instance:
  • Players develop relationships and online communities that form into cultures with their own social norms and with social consequences for behaving badly by harrassing others, even while anonymous.
  • Players customize their avatars' appearance in virtual environments to:
    • reflect who they are
    • maximize their identification with their avatars
    • and manage the way others perceive them.
  • The way users' avatars look influences how they behave in virtual space and in the real world.
  • This effect can be manipulated for users' benefit in virtual therapy.
  • Unfortunately, the psychological impact of using avatars can also exacerbate social problems like sexism and racism, in some situations.
An avatar is a digital icon or figure representing a particular person in virtual worlds, such as online video games. ?
Relevant virtual worlds have three characteristics: 1
  • Real-time conversation
  • Fully customizable virtual self-presentations (avatars)
  • A representative and immersive 3D world
Usually, players spend time in virtual worlds for a combination of the following reasons: 1
  • To build personal relationships
  • To earn money
  • To escape emotionally or mentally from their current surroundings; or rather, become immersed in another world
  • To learn
